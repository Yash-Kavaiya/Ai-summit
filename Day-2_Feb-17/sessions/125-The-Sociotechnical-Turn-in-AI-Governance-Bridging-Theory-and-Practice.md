## üìù Summary

This session will explore how sociotechnical approaches to artificial intelligence can move from theory to real-world governance practice. It will examine how social context, power, trust, and institutional design shape AI risks and outcomes beyond purely technical solutions. Bringing together research and practice, the discussion will focus on translating sociotechnical insights into actionable frameworks for safe and trusted AI, with particular relevance for public sector deployment and Global South contexts.
